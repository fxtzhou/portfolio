The Dot Com Bubble

October 30, 2017


During the 'Dot Com Bubble' around 1997-2001, a lot of excessive speculation occurred around the Internet. It started gaining traction and people started to adapt and use the Internet heavily. It was also used by businesses and consumers. Around 2000-2002 was when a lot of companies that took part of utilizing the Internet failed. Big corporations we now know to be Amazon.com and eBay failed during this time as well, and then well-surpassed their stocks in a later recovery.
Naturally, the Internet usage started to pick up due to the creation of the World Wide Web. Increases in Internet availability, increases of uses for the Internet, and increase of knowledge of the Internet naturally also led to more users of the Internet. Many companies and investors wanted to be a part of this, that is getting their company onto the web so that users could be exposed to their products, and naturally began to invest in ".com" sites. This created a stock market bubble - a huge increase in supply of these '.com' sites, and not a whole lot of demand at that time.
Another reason that attributed to the bust was that these companies adopted a mindset of "go big or go home," or "get big fast." They started to offer their services or products for free in hopes that brand awareness would eventually allow them to charge higher rates for their products. Once they embraced this mentality of "growth over profits," and started to plan solely on the future of their products, they started to realize that a lot of their losses were picking up due to their offers of free or discounted products.
